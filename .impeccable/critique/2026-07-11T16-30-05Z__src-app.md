## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: Passes the slop test. No eyebrows, no card grids, no gradient text, no cream default. Young Serif + Hanken Grotesk pairs on a real contrast axis, and the fountain-ink concept (ink-blue ramp, apricot decoration budget) gives it an identity you couldn't guess from "developer portfolio". Slight second-order watch: the hand-drawn squiggle is itself becoming a known "human touch" gesture — here it's held to two instances, which keeps it voice rather than grammar. Keep it that way.

**Deterministic scan**: CLI scan of `src/` + `mdx-components.tsx`: 0 findings (was 2 pre-redesign). In-browser scan of all four pages: home and blog list clean; `/projects` flags 4× line-length (~89 chars/line on the work notes, aim <80); blog post flags one skipped heading (h1 → h3, the markdown uses `###` directly under the title).

**Visual overlays**: injection ran headless (no user-visible tab); findings reported from console output instead.

## Overall Impression

Warm, calm, and personal — it reads as designed by a person, and the discipline (one squiggle, one wavy rule, accent ≤10%) is what makes it work. The biggest remaining problems aren't visual: the copy contradicts itself about where Hritik works now, and a portfolio for someone who builds visual, animation-heavy mobile UIs never shows a single pixel of that work.

## What's Working

- **The identity is specific.** Ink-blue ramp + apricot decoration + bookish serif = "fountain pen letter", and every page keeps the same voice. Contrast is genuinely excellent (body 14.5:1, worst secondary text 5.4:1).
- **Restraint with a pulse.** One orchestrated entrance on home, quiet fades elsewhere, reduced-motion fully handled (durations *and* delays zeroed).
- **Low cognitive load.** Single column, ≤4 items per group, one action per row, metadata right-aligned and tabular.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] The copy contradicts itself about the present.** Intro says "currently at BaaziGames", but the Work list shows BharatPe as most recent (Sep 2025 – Jan 2026 — already ended), and `layout.tsx` metadata says "4+ years" while the page says "5+ years".
  **Why it matters**: recruiters cross-check; a contradiction on the hero erodes exactly the trust the site exists to build.
  **Fix**: one source of truth for current role + years; update intro, metadata description, and the work list together.
  **Suggested command**: /impeccable clarify

- **[P1] The work is never shown.** A mobile engineer whose selling points are "fast and smooth", Reanimated-heavy game tables, and design systems — presented entirely as text. Brand register: a portfolio that implies imagery must ship imagery.
  **Why it matters**: "I make things feel fast and smooth" is a claim; a 10-second screen recording or device-frame screenshots is proof. Visitors remember what they saw.
  **Fix**: add app screenshots / device mockups / short recordings to the projects page (even 2–3 hero shots).
  **Suggested command**: /impeccable shape projects imagery

- **[P2] Navigation labels don't match destinations.** Nav says "projects" → page titled "Work"; nav "blog" → "Writing". Home's "Work" section links to `/projects`. No active-page indicator in the header.
  **Why it matters**: recognition over recall — three names for the same place makes visitors re-orient.
  **Fix**: pick one vocabulary ("work" / "writing" everywhere), add a subtle current-page state in the nav.
  **Suggested command**: /impeccable clarify

- **[P2] Projects notes run ~89 chars/line** (detector-confirmed, ×4). At 14px across the full 672px column, the notes are wider than comfortable reading measure.
  **Fix**: cap the note paragraphs at ~65ch (`max-w-[60ch]`).
  **Suggested command**: /impeccable layout

- **[P2] Small touch target on the theme switcher.** ~28px effective target (p-1.5 + 16px icon); Apple/Android guidelines want ≥44px. Blog post also has a skipped heading level (h1 → h3 in the markdown).
  **Fix**: enlarge the toggle's padding; change `###` to `##` in the two posts.
  **Suggested command**: /impeccable adapt

## Persona Red Flags

**Riya (recruiter, from PRODUCT.md audience)**: No resume/CV link anywhere; LinkedIn exists only as a small footer link; the "currently at BaaziGames" contradiction is exactly what she'd catch while cross-checking against LinkedIn. She leaves with a question mark instead of a phone screen.

**Casey (distracted mobile user)**: Theme toggle is a ~28px target near the screen edge — misses will hit "blog". On mobile, tapping "projects" lands on a jobs list; the side projects (the actual clickable artifacts) are below the fold with no hint they exist.

**Jordan (first-timer)**: Identity is clear within 5 seconds (name, role, employers — genuinely good). Only stumble: clicks "projects" expecting projects, gets a work-history page titled "Work".

## Minor Observations

- Home's "Say hello" section and the footer repeat the same three links within one viewport.
- The view counter POSTs on every page load — author visits and refreshes inflate counts.
- Avatar (github.com) and company favicons (google.com/s2) are external dependencies; fine, but they'll show broken/blank if blocked.
- Mixed link grammar in "Say hello": email gets the accent underline, twitter/github get muted text. Reads as deliberate emphasis; keep or unify consciously.
- No RSS feed for the writing section.
