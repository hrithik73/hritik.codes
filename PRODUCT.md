# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and fellow engineers who land here from a resume, X, or GitHub. They're scanning: who is this person, where have they worked, can they build. Secondary: readers arriving at a blog post from search. Both are on a quick visit — the site must communicate warmth and competence in under a minute.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Hritik Singh, a mobile (React Native) engineer with 5+ years at BharatPe, Baazi Games, and CARS24. It exists to make a memorable, human first impression and to host occasional writing. Success = a visitor remembers the site felt personal and crafted, and can recall what Hritik does.

## Brand Personality

Warm, handwritten, unhurried. The mood: a handwritten letter in blue-black fountain ink on a bright page. Personal correspondence, not a corporate landing page. Calm confidence — the work speaks in plain sentences, not superlatives.

## Anti-references

- SaaS-template portfolios: hero metrics, card grids, gradient text.
- The AI-default look: cream/beige backgrounds, uppercase tracked eyebrow labels above every section, uniform fade-up entrances on everything.
- Cold "developer terminal" aesthetics: monospace-everything, graph-paper grids, neon-on-black.

## Design Principles

1. **Ink on a bright page** — warmth lives in typography and the accent, never in a tinted background.
2. **One organic gesture per surface** — a hand-drawn squiggle or wavy rule earns its place once; repeated, it becomes scaffolding.
3. **Readable over elegant** — no text below 4.5:1 contrast; body copy at 16px. Small gray type is not restraint.
4. **Plain sentences, sentence case** — section headings and copy read like speech, not labels.
5. **Motion is a greeting, not a system** — one orchestrated entrance on the home page; everything else stays still.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: all text ≥4.5:1 (≥3:1 for large text). Full keyboard navigation with visible focus rings, skip link, `prefers-reduced-motion` honored everywhere (including the theme-switcher click sound). Light and dark themes both first-class.
